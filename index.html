Chocolate Cake

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 10 minutes prep, 35 minutes cooking, 45 minutes total
  • Serves: 8

Chocolate cake

Ingredients Quantity
Plain flour 1 3/4 cups
Cocoa powder 3/4 cups
Baking powder 1 1/2 tsp
Baking soda 1 1/2 tsp
White sugar 2 cups
Salt 1 tsp
Eggs 2
Milk 1 cup
Vanilla extract 2 tsp
Milk 1 cup
Boiling water 1 cup
Vegetable oil 1/2 cup
Chocolat buttercream frosting 2 batches

Method

Step 1: Preheat oven to 180 degrees celsius

Step 2: Grease 2 cake pans with butter, then line the base

Step 3: Sift flour, cocoa, baking powder and baking soda into a large bowl. Add Sugar and salt. Whisk briefly to combine.

Step 4: Add eggs, milk, oil and vanilla. Whisk well to combine until lump free - about 30 seconds

Step 5: Add boiling water and whisk to incorporate.

Step 6: Pour batter into cake pans.

Step 7: Bake for 35 minutes or until a wooden skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean

Step 8: Cool for 10 minutes, then turn out onto wire racks upside down.

Step 9: Cool completely before frosting with Chocolate Buttercream Frosting.

Strawberry ice cream

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 20 minutes prep, 1 hr cooking, 1hr 20 mins total
  • Serves: 6

Strawberry ice cream

Ingredients Quantity
Strawberries 1 kg
White sugar 1 tbsp
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Sweetened condensed milk 1 can
Cream (full fat) 2 cups

Method

Step 1: Preheat oven to 160 degrees celsius with a shelf in the middle

Step 2: Pile strawberries on a tray. Sprinkle over sugar. Toss through, then spread strawberries out on tray.

Step 3: Roast for 45 minutes, rotating tray halfway. Remove from oven, toss strawberries, then return to oven for 15 minutes. Keep an eye on it

Step 4: Remove from oven, use spatula to scrape into a food processor, being sure to get all the juices and caramelised bits off the tray.

Step 5: Blitz until smooth as possible (caramelised bits won't blitz smooth), scrape into bowl then cool completely.

Step 6: Add condensed milk and vanilla into strawberries. Beat well for 1 1/2 minutes on high to aerate and combine.

Step 7: Place cream in another bowl. Use electric beater to beat into stiff peaks - about 3 minutes on high.

Step 8: Add a dollop of cream into the strawberry mixture. Fold through gently, don't beat in furiously When mostly mixed through, add another dollop and for through about 5 times.

Step 9: Tip strawberry mixture into the cream bowl. Gently fold through until it's a uniform pink colour - about 15 to 20 folds max. Some streaks is ok.

Step 10: Pour mixture into a container (preferably glass with airtight lid), smooth top.

Step 11: Place sheet of baking paper on surface and press out air bubbles (stops icicles forming). Cover with lid, freeze 12+ hours.

Step 12: Remove from freezer, remove paper. Scoop with ice cream scooper and serve in cones or bowls!

Chocolate mousse

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 20 minutes prep
  • Serves: 4

Chocolate mousse

Ingredients Quantity
Eggs 3
Dark chocolate 125g
Unsalted butter 10g
Cream (full fat) 1/2 cup
Caster sugar 3 tbsp

Method

Step 1: Separate eggs and yolks while eggs are cold. Place whites in a large bowl and yolks in a small bowl. Leave whites while you prepare other ingredients.

Step 2: Whisk egg yolks.

Step 3: Place chocolate and butter in a bowl. Melt in the microwave in 30 second bursts, stirring in between, until smooth. Set aside to cool

Step 4: Beat cream until stiff peaks form

Step 5: Add sugar. Beat whites until firm peaks form

Step 6: Fold egg yolks into cream using a rubber spatula - 8 folds max. Streaks is ok.

Step 7: Touch the chocolate. Should still be runny but only lukewarm. If too thick, microwave 2 x 3 seconds until runny.

Step 8: Pour chocolate into cream yolk mixture. Fold through - 8 folds max. Streaks ok.

Step 9: Add 1/4 of beaten egg whites into chocolate mixture. Fold through until incorporated - "smear" the spatular across surface to blend white lumps in - aim for 10 folds.

Step 10: Pour chocolate mixture into egg whites. Fold through until incorporated and no more white lumps remain - aim for 12 folds max.

Step 11: Divide mixture between 4 small glasses or pots. Refrigerate for at least 5 hours, preferably overnight.

Step 12: To serve, garnish with cream and chocolate shavings. Raspberries and a tiny sprig of mint for colour would also be lovely!

Tiramisu

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 30 minutes prep
  • Serves: 6

Tiramisu

Ingredients Quantity
Eggs 3
Vanilla extract 1/2 tsp
Caster sugar 1/2 cup
Mascarpone 250g
Black coffee 1 1/4 cups
Frangelico/Kahlua 2 tbsp
Lady finger/pavesini/savoiardi biscuits 200g
Cocoa For dusting

Method

Step 1: Beat yolks and sugar in stand mixer or electric beater on speed 6 to 7 for 10 minutes or until it changes from yellow to pale yellow (almost white), and is thick (see video for consistency).

Step 2: Add vanilla and mascarpone, beat until just combined and smooth. Transfer mixture to a bowl, set aside

Step 3: Clean bowl and whisk. Add egg whites and beat until it's stiff (3 minutes on speed 7)

Step 4: Add 1/2 the cream mixture into the egg whites. Gently fold through. When most incorporated, add the remaining cream mixture and fold through until just combined - don't bash out all the air in the egg whites!

Step 5: Mix coffee and liquor together. Quickly dip biscuits in and line the bottom of a 8”/20cm square dish.

Step 6: Spread over half the cream, then top with another layer of coffee dipped biscuits.

Step 7: Spread with remaining cream.

Step 8: Cover, refrigerate for at least 4 - 5 hours, preferably overnight

Step 9: Dust with cocoa powder just before serving - either before you cut or after placing onto serving plates.

Berry and meringue tart

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 25 minutes prep, 1 hr 15 mins cooking, 1hr 40 mins total
  • Serves: 16

Berry and meringue tart

Ingredients Quantity
Egg whites 180mL
Caster sugar 300g
Heavy whipping cream 600mL
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Caster sugar 3 tbsp
Mangoes 2
Strawberries 500g
Raspberries and blueberries 450 g

Method

Step 1: Preheat oven to 120C/250F (standard) or 100C/210F (fan/convection).

Step 2: Mark 2 rectangles 11 x 40 cm / 4.5 x 15" on a sheet of baking paper using pencil, with 2.5cm / 1" between each outline. Repeat with another sheet of paper (so 4 outlines).

Step 3: Find 2 baking trays large enough so the outline fits on the tray surface when the tray is turned upside down (rim gets in way of piping).

Step 4: Fit a large piping bag with a 11mm / 0.5" round nozzle

Step 5: Separate the egg whites while the eggs are fridge cold.

Step 6: Place whites in a large clean, dry bowl. Whip until it all turns into foam and there's no liquid whites left (1 minute on speed 8)

Step 7: With the beater still going, add the sugar one heaped tablespoon at a time - about 1 minute.

Step 8: After the sugar is added, crank the beater up to speed 10 and whip for 1 minute. Fluff should be glossy and when you rub between fingers, there should be barely any sugar grit - a bit is ok.

Step 9: Spoon half the meringue into piping bag

Step 10: Dab small amount on the corner of an upside down baking tray. Place paper on tray pencil marking side down.

Step 11: Pipe filling inside the lines - don't worry if there are gaps. Use dessert spoon to smooth surface - the base should be about 1/2 cm thick (rises to ~7 mm cooked).

Step 12: Pipe blobs along one short end (to make tart ends) and all along both long sides. Do this to both on Tray 1. Place in oven.

Step 13: Repeat with remaining meringues on Tray 2, but do not pipe blobs along the short end (these will be the middle pieces). Place in oven on shelf just below Tray 1.

Step 14: Use any remaining meringue to make extra small blobs (good for repairs / decoration / nibbling). Place this tray on the floor of oven.

Step 15: Bake for 45 minutes, then swap the two trays (tray with blobs not a priority, leave it!).

Step 16: Bake for further 30 minutes, then turn off the oven, keep door closed and leave to cool (can keep in oven even for days). Meringue should be dry and crisp, able to lift off paper.

Step 17: Place cream, vanilla and sugar in a bowl and whip until soft peaks form.

Step 18: Trim joining ends of meringues with knife so they are straight.

Step 19: Dab cream on cut end and join meringues. Assemble where you plan to serve.

Step 20: Spread over cream, top with fruit, dust with sugar. Serve immediately, and puff up with pride when lavished with compliments!

Strawberry crumble

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 15 mins prep, 35 mins cooking, 50 mins total
  • Serves: 6

Strawberry crumble

Ingredients Quantity
Strawberries 1 kg
White sugar 1/3 - 1/2 cup
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Rolled oats 3/4 cup
White flour 3/4 cup
Brown sugar 3/4 cup
Baking powder 1/2 tsp
Cinammon powder 3/4 tsp
Unsalted butter 90g
Vanilla ice cream To serve
Salt A pinch
Water 2 tbsp

Method

Step 1: Preheat oven to 180C/350F.

Step 2: Place all ingredients except butter in a bowl. Mix to combine. Add butter and mix to incorporate.

Step 3: Hull / cut top off strawberries. Cut larger ones in half, leave small ones whole (2cm / 4/5" height).

Step 4: Place strawberries in bowl. Scatter over cornflour, toss well to coat evenly.

Step 5: Add sugar, water and vanilla. Mix well.

Step 6: Pour strawberries into a baking pan. Crumble over topping, using your fingers to get some nice crumbly chunks.

Step 7: Bake for 30 to 40 minutes until the Topping is deep golden and the strawberry syrup is bubbling up energetically all around the edges, this is a key sign that the strawberry sauce has thickened sufficiently.

Step 8: Stand for a few minutes before serving with ice cream or your choice of topping.

Basque cheesecake

Ingredients Quantity
Cream cheese (preferably Philadelphia) 1kg
Caster sugar 1 1/2 cups (330g)
Australian Free Range eggs 6
Thickened cream 600 mL
Plain flour 1/3 cup (50 g)
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Salt 1 tsp

Method

Step 1: Preheat the oven to the 230 degrees celsius

Step 2: Line a 6-inch cake pan with 2.5-inch sides with baking paper. If the pan has a removable bottom, you can use the bottom to press the paper into the pan. Then you can use your hands to crease the sides to hold its shape. Once the paper is molded to the pan, you can remove the bottom and the paper and then reattach the bottom to the pan, placing the paper on top.

Step 3: Add all of the ingredients to a blender and blend until smooth

Step 4: Pour the mixture into the prepared cake pan

Step 5: Bake the cheesecake until the top is just shy of turning black. The cake should still be very jiggly in the center when you remove it from the oven.

Step 6: Let the cheesecake cool on a cooling rack and then place it in a sealable container and refrigerate overnight.

Step 7: Slice and serve with fruit (optional)

Strawberry cheesecake

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 30 minutes prep, 55 mins cooking
  • Serves: 12

strawberry-cheesecake

Ingredients Quantity
Cheesecake biscuit base
Arnott's Marie crackers 200g
Unsalted butter, melted 120g
Cheesecake filling
Cream cheese 500g
Plain flour 2 tbsp
Sour cream 1/2 cup
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Eggs 3
Caster sugar 1 1/2 cups
Lemon zest 1 lemon
Strawberry topping
Strawberries, half diced and half halved 500g
Lemon juice or water 2 tbsp
White sugar 1/2 cup
Vanilla extract 1/2 tsp
Cornflour/cornstarch 1 1/2 tsp
Water 2 tbsp

Method

Preparation

Step 1: Preheat oven to 160C/320F (standard) or 140C/295F (fan/convection)

Step 2: Get a 20cm/8″ springform cake tin. Turn the base upside down, butter lightly and place a square piece of parchment/baking paper on the base. Then clip into the springform pan – excess paper will stick out.

Step 3: Butter and line the side of the pan.

Cheesecake biscuit base

Step 4: Break up biscuits roughly by hand and place in a food processor.

Step 5: Blitz until fine crumbs. Add butter, briefly blitz until dispersed and it resembles wet sand.

Step 6: Pour into the prepared cake tin. Use a spatula to roughly spread it out over the base and up the walls.

Step 7: Use something with a flat base and vertical edges (e.g. measuring cup) to press the crumbs up the wall almost to the top of the sides, and flatten the base.

Filling

Step 8: Use a mixer or beater to beat the cream cheese until just smooth, no longer than 20 seconds on speed 4. (Do not aerate cheesecake filling as it causes cracks when it bakes)

Step 9: Add flour, beat for 5 seconds on speed 4 until just incorporated.

Step 10: Add vanilla, sour cream, sugar and lemon zest. Beat until just combined (10 sec max, speed 5).

Step 11: Add eggs one at a time, beat in between until just combined (5 sec each), and after the last one, beat just until the egg is fully incorporated.

Step 12: Pour into prepared crust.

Step 13: Bake for 55 minutes. The top should be a very light golden brown, not cracked, and near perfectly flat. It should jiggle slightly when you gently shake the pan.

Step 14: Cool the cake in the oven with the door open 20 cm / 8", then refrigerate for 4 hours+ in the pan.

Step 15: Remove sides. Use overhang paper to slide cheesecake off the cake pan. Then slide the cheesecake off the paper.

Strawberry topping

Step 16: Place chopped strawberries, vanilla, sugar and lemon juices in a saucepan. Stir then bring to simmer over medium heat.

Step 17: Simmer for 10 minutes until strawberries breakdown.

Step 18: Mix cornflour with water, then add into saucepan and stir.

Step 19: Add halved strawberries and cook for 1 minute to soften.

Step 20: Sauce should be syrupy – remove from stove and cool. Will thicken as it cools.

Step 21: Once cooled, stir. Adjust thickness to make it the right “oozing” consistency with a tiny touch of water (be careful!).

Step 22: Spoon onto cheesecake so it’s covered with a single layer of strawberries. Flip strawberry halves so they are facedown. Refrigerate 2 hours+.

Step 23: Slice and serve with remaining Strawberry Sauce!

Profiteroles

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 30 minutes prep, 40 mins cooking
  • Serves: 48

Profiteroles

Ingredients Quantity
Choux Pastry
Unsalted butter 100g
Water 1 cup
Flour 1 cup
Large eggs 4
Salt Pinch
Thick custard filling
Egg yolks 4
White sugar 1/4 cup
Cornflour/cornstarch 3 1/2 tbsp
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Milk (full fat recommended) 2 1/3 cups
Extra white sugar 1/4 cups
Chocolate sauce
Dark chocolate 250g
Cream 1 cup
Filling options (choose one)
Vanilla custard (Creme Patissiere)
Ice cream
Whipped cream

Method

Step 1: Place butter in a saucepan over medium heat. When mostly melted, add water then bring to a rapid simmer.

Step 2: While liquid is simmering, add flour and pinch of salt, and stir vigorously with a wooden spoon (still on stove) until the dough forms sort of a ball and pulls away from the sides of the saucepan. About 1 minute.

Step 3: Remove from heat and let mixture cool for 10 minutes.

Step 4: Preheat oven to 220°C/420°F (200°C fan).

Step 5: Lightly grease 2 large or 3 standard baking trays with butter, then line with paper. (Grease stops paper sliding)

Step 6: Add 1 egg into batter, beat until combined. Add remaining eggs one at a time making sure each is combined before adding the next. Dough should be like a thick paste.

Step 7: Place dough into a piping bag fitted with a 13 – 15mm round tip (1/2 – 3/5").

Filling

Step 8: Pipe 2 tsp blobs 5cm/2" apart (I do 16 on each tray). Makes 40 – 50 blobs.

Step 9: Wet your finger with water and pat down any peaks to form domes (for nice round balls).

Step 10: Bake 2 trays at a time for 15 minutes, switching the trays halfway.

Step 11: Remove from oven, reduce heat to 180°C/35°0F (160°C fan). They should be crispy enough that when pierced, they will not collapse – if not, return to oven for 3 minutes.

Step 12: Pierce balls with knife then return to oven for 5 – 7 minutes until golden and crispy.

Step 13: Transfer to rack, cool fully before filling with custard, cream or split then fill with ice cream. Drizzle with chocolate and serve!

Chocolate topping

Step 1: Place cream and chocolate in a heatproof bowl. Microwave 4 x 30 seconds, stirring in between, until incorporated. Cool to warm, then use for drizzling or dipping.

Custard filling

Step 1: Whisk yolks and sugar, then whisk in cornflour.

Step 2: Place milk, vanilla and extra sugar in a saucepan over medium high. Heat until just before simmering.

Step 3: Whisk in 1/4 cup hot milk into egg mixture. Whisk in another 1/4 cup. Then add remaining milk mixture and whisk.

Step 4: Pour back into saucepan. Return to stove on medium low (low for strong stoves). Whisk constantly 2 to 3 minutes until the mixture thicks to a thick dolloping custard consistency.

Step 5: Remove from heat immediately, pour into bowl. Press cling wrap onto surface (stops skin forming) then refrigerate for 4 to 5 hours until set. Do not whisk again once set (loses thickness).

Step 6: Spoon into piping bag fitting with a very fine nozzle. Then pipe into profiteroles (use the hole pierced during bake, or make a new hole).

Green Tea (Matcha) Cake with White Chocolate Frosting

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 30 minutes prep, 15 mins cooking
  • Serves: 8

Green tea cake

Ingredients Quantity
Green tea cake
Unsalted butter, softened 1 cup
Granulated sugar 1 cup
Flour 1 cup
Baking powder 2 tsp
Egg yolks 3
Egg whites 3
Matcha powder 1 1/2 tbsp
Milk 2 tbsp
White chocolate frosting
Unsalted butter, softened 1 cup
Confectioner's sugar 1 1/2 cup
White chocolate, melted 6 tbsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Milk 1 tsp

Method

Step 1: Preheat oven at 375 degrees Fahrenheit. Grease the bottom and the sides of two 20-cm diameter with melted butter. Cover the bottom and sides of both pans with parchment paper, and grease the parchment papers once again with melted butter. Set aside.

Step 2: In a large mixing bowl, mix the softened butter and granulated sugar using an electric mixer until well combined and soft. Add in the egg yolks and continue mixing using the electric mixer until well combined and creamy.

Step 3: In a separate bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and matcha powder using a whisk. Pass the mixture through a sieve to make it uniform. Add the flour mixture into the butter-sugar-egg yolk mixture 1-2 tablespoons at a time and fold using a rubber spatula until they are well combined. If the batter is too dry to fold, add a couple of tablespoons of milk to moisten it up.

Step 4: In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites using an electric mixer until it reaches soft peaks. Add the egg whites 1/3 at a time into the cake batter and fold gently using a rubber spatula.

Step 5: Pour half of the cake batter into one baking pan, and the other half into the other baking pan. Bake at 375 degrees Fahrenheit for 12-15 minutes. To check, stick a toothpick in the middle of the cake. If the toothpick comes out clean, then the cake is ready. When the cakes are baked, remove immediately from the pans and let cool at room temperature.

Step 6: To make the white chocolate frosting, mix together the butter, confectioner's sugar, melted white chocolate, salt, and milk in a large mixing bowl using an electric mixer until well combined and creamy. Refrigerate if it's not being used immediately.

Step 7: To assemble the cake, place one cake layer on a flat surface and spread the frosting on top. Place another cake layer on top of the frosting. Finish by covering the top and sides with frosting. Top with matcha powder.

Apple strudel

Ingredients Quantity
Green apples, cored and thinly sliced 6
Lemon juice 1 tbsp
Sugar 1/3 cup (80g)
Dry white breadcrumbs 1/2 cup (50g)
Ground almonds 2/3 cup
Ground cinnamon 1 tsp
Filo/puff pastry 8 sheets
Butter, melted 100g
Icing sugar for dusting
Cream or ice cream for serving

Method

Step 1: Preheat the oven to 180C and lightly grease a baking tray.

Step 2: Put the apple, lemon juice, sugar and breadcrumbs in a large bowl, and mix well.

Step 3: Combine the almonds and cinnamon in a separate bowl.

Step 4: Lay one sheet filo pastry horizontally on a work surface and brush with melted butter. Lay another sheet over it so that it overlaps the first sheet on the edge furthest from you by 5cm. Brush the second sheet then sprinkle with a quarter of the almond mixture. Repeat until you have used all the filo and almond mixture.

Step 5: Place the apple mixture along the edge closest to you, leaving a 4cm border on three sides.

Step 6: Carefully roll up, folding the sides over about halfway, then roll to the end.

Step 7: Place the strudel on the prepared baking tray, seam-side-down. Brush with melted butter and bake for 40 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown.

Step 8: Sprinkle with icing sugar and serve with cream or ice-cream.

Red velvet cupcakes

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 30 minutes prep, 20 mins cooking
  • Serves: 12

Red velvet cupcake

Ingredients Quantity
Dry ingredients
Cake flour 1 1/3 cups
Cocoa powder 1 tbsp
Baking powder 1 1/2 tsp
Salt A pinch
Wet ingredients
Unsalted butter 4 tbsp (60g)
Caster sugar 2/3 cup
Large eggs 2
Vegetable oil 1/3 cup
Buttermilk) 1/2 cup
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
White vinegar 1/2 tsp
Red food colouring 1 tbsp
Cream cheese frosting
Unsalted butter 1/3 cup
Philadelphia block cream cheese 170g
Vanilla extract 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/8 tsp
Soft icing sugar mixture 2 1/2 cups

Method

Cupcakes

Step 1: Preheat oven to 180°C/350°F (160°C fan). Line a standard 12 hole muffin tin with cupcake liners.

Step 2: Sift dry ingredients together into a bowl.

Step 3: Put the butter in a separate bowl and beat on speed 2 for 1 minute with an electric mixer or stand mixer (paddle attachment).

Step 4: Add sugar then beat for a further 2 minutes, same speed, until the butter is very pale yellow, almost white.

Step 5: Add the eggs one at a time beating for 30 seconds on speed 1 after each addition.

Step 6: Add oil, buttermilk, vanilla and red food colouring then beat on Speed 1 until incorporated and the batter is smooth.

Step 7: Sprinkle the dry ingredients across the surface and mix on Speed 1 for just 20 seconds. Scrape down sides of the bowl, then mix again for 10 seconds. The batter should now be smooth – few small lumps is ok. Do not keep beating – overworks batter = cupcakes not as soft!

Filling

Step 8: Divide the batter between the 12 cupcake liners – it should fill 3/4 of the way, but it depends on the size of the cupcake liners you use.

Step 9: Bake for 20 minutes or until a skewer inserted into the centre of the middle cupcake comes out clean.

Step 10: Immediately remove cupcakes onto a wire rack. Cool completely before frosting with cream cheese frosting.

Frosting

Step 11: Place butter in a bowl and beat for 1 minute until it's smooth and starts to become paler in colour – speed 6 on stand mixer, speed 9 for electric beater.

Step 12: Add cream cheese then beat for a further 1 minute on the same speed until smooth.

Step 13: Add icing sugar in 4 batches, beating in between until incorporated, starting on speed 1 so you don't get a dust storm in your face!

Step 14: Add vanilla and salt, then beat for 2 minutes until fluffy – speed 6 with stand mixer, speed 9 with electric beater. It should be creamy but still hold its shape in a peak. If it's too sloppy, place in fridge for 30 minutes, then beat again.

Step 15: Transfer into piping bag with desired piping tip. Pipe onto cupcakes – makes enough to generously frost 12 cupcakes.

Step 16: Serve!

Chocolate brownies

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 10 minutes prep, 25 mins cooking
  • Serves: 9

Chocolate brownie

Ingredients Quantity
Unsalted butter 200 g (14 tbsp)
Dark chocolate chips 200 g (1 1/4 cups)
Brown sugar 1 cup (175 g)
Eggs, lightly beaten 3
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Plain flour 1/2 cup (75g)
Cocoa powder 1/4 cup (30g)
Salt 1 pinch
Dark chocolate block/bar (optional) 180g
Whipped cream/ice cream for serving

Method

Step 1: Preheat oven to 180°C/350°F (160°C fan forced).

Step 2: Spray a 20cm/8" square tin with oil and line with baking/parchment paper with overhang.

Step 3: Place butter and chocolate chips in a heatproof bowl, microwave in 30 second bursts (takes me 1m 30 sec) until melted. Stir until smooth.

Step 4: Add sugar and vanilla, mix, then add eggs and mix well until smooth and molten.

Step 5: Add flour, cocoa and salt and stir until smooth. Stir in chopped chocolate, pour into pan.

Step 6: Bake 24 minutes for really gooey in the centre, 28 minutes for fudgey but still very moist, 32 minutes for moist fudge-cake-like.

Pavlova

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 10 minutes prep, 1hr 30mins mins cooking
  • Serves: 10

Pavlova

Ingredients Quantity
Egg whites 150mL
Caster sugar 1 cup/220g
Cornflour/cornstarch, sifted 1 tbsp
White vinegar 1 tsp
Cream
Thickened/heavy crean 1 1/2 cups (375mL)
Caster sugar 1/4 cup/55g
Vanilla extract or essence 1 tsp
Topping
Fruit of choice: raspberries, blueberries, strawberries, cherries

Method

Preparation

Step 1: Separate the whites and yolks while eggs are cold. Measure 150 ml/5oz egg whites (140 - 160ml is ok).

Step 2: Set whites aside to come to room temperature.

Step 3: Preheat oven to 150C/300F (fan / convection) / 170C/340F (standard).

Step 4: Place whites in a bowl. Use a stand mixer (I use speed 7) or handheld beater (high) and beat until soft peaks form

Step 5: Add sugar 1 tbsp at a time, beating as you go.

Step 6: After adding the sugar, beat for a further 3 minutes or until thick and glossy. Rub a little of the fluff between your fingers, there should be no sugar grit - that means it is ready.

Step 7: Add cornflour and vinegar, beat on low for 5 - 7 seconds (or fold through with spatula) until just mixed through.

Making the pavlova

Step 8: Get the base of a springform cake pan (24cm/9.5" or larger) and turn it upside down. Dab meringue on the edge and place a piece of baking paper (parchment paper) on top.

Step 9: Gently place half the fluff onto the paper. Use the cake pan as a guide to make it round and coax it into a circle shape around 20cm / 8" in diameter.

Step 10: Carefully scoop out the remaining fluff. Now coax it into a dome shape (not a cake with straight smooth sides) with edges sloping in slightly. Flatten top. Make it 4-5cm / 2 " high - not much higher.

Step 11: Transfer to baking tray. CAREFULLY place in the oven, GENTLY close the door and turn oven DOWN to 100C/210F (fan / convection) / 115C/240F (standard).

Step 12: Bake for 1 1/2 hours

Step 13: Turn oven off, leave the door closed and leave pavlova in the oven overnight to cool

Step 14: Transfer pavlova to serving platter then slide cake pan out from underneath. Use butter knife if required to loosen edges from paper, then slide paper out from underneath.

Step 15: Just before serving, top with cream and fruit of choice.

Step 16:Serve!

Topping

Step 1: Place cream, sugar and vanilla in a bowl. Beat cream until it is thickened and just holds its shape - don't overbeat, it should be silky smooth, not speckled with bubbles and stiff.

Gingerbread men

  • Go back to contents
  • Time: 30 minutes prep, 40 mins cooking
  • Serves: 18

Gingerbread man

Ingredients Quantity
Dry ingredients
Flour 3 cups
Ground ginger 1 tbsp
Baking powder 3 tsp
Ground cinnamon 2 tsp
Mixed spice 1 tsp
Ground cloves 1/4 tsp
Wet ingredients
Unsalted butter, softened 5 1/2 tbsp (85g)
Brown sugar 3/4 cup
Large eggs 1
Vegetable oil 1/3 cup
Vanilla extract 2 tsp
Molasses 1/2 cup
Premade icing

Method

Step 1: Whisk dry ingredients on one bowl

Step 2: In a separate bowl, cream the butter and sugar together using a stand mixer or electric beater on speed 7 for 2 minutes, or until light and fluffy.

Step 3: Add the egg and vanilla, then mix on speed 5 until combined (~30 sec).

Step 4: Add the molasses, mix for 1 minute using speed 2.

Step 5: Add 1/4 of the Dry ingredients, then mix until you can't see flour (start on speed 1 to stop flour flying everywhere!). Repeat until all Dry ingredients are mixed in, then mix for further 15 seconds. Dough should be soft and a bit sticky.

Step 6: Pat dough into a disc shape, wrap in the cling wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or until firm.

Step 7: Preheat the oven to 375°F/190°C (170°C fan) and set a shelf in the middle. Line 3 x baking trays with parchment/baking paper.

Step 8: Take the dough out of the refrigerator and cut into half. Wrap half and return to the fridge.

Step 9: Sprinkle a sheet of baking paper very lightly with flour. Place dough on top, sprinkle surface very lightly with flour. Place another sheet of baking paper on top. Roll dough out until 4mm / 1/6" thick using a rolling pin. As you work, flip and if paper is wrinkling, peel it off then put if back on, smoothing out wrinkles.

Step 10: Use a gingerbread cookie cutter to cut out gingerbread men shapes, firmly pressing to cut all the way through. If the dough sticks to the cutter, dip it into flour.

Step 11: Use a palette knife (or large knife) to transfer shapes onto baking sheet. Place 6 on the first sheet. Tidy edges if necessary using a butter knife.

Step 12: Bake for 12 minutes (for classic light golden) or 14 minutes (dark golden and crisp). Then remove and cool fully on tray.

Step 13: Gather scraps of dough, press together then roll out again. If dough is sticky, return to refrigerator and work on the other half of the dough instead. Repeat rolling out and cutting out. You should have 18 to 20 gingerbread men in total (depending on cutter size).

Step 14: Fill and bake 2nd and 3rd tray with gingerbread men, 6 to 8 on each tray. I find the workflow that works best is baking one tray at a time ie. roll/cut-out/fill/bake one tray while you prepare the next.

Step 15: Fully cool on trays, then decorate! Use a half batch of this any premade icing to pipe and stick on decorations (sets hard and doesn't soak into cookies).

Step 16: Serve!
